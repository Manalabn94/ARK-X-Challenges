Victor Hugo


Biography of Victor Hugo :

Victor Hugo, the renowned French writer, stands as a towering figure in the world of literature. Born on February 26, 1802, in Besançon, Hugo's literary genius and impassioned activism left an indelible mark on both French and global culture. His literary journey spans a diverse range of genres, from poetry to novels, plays, and essays, earning him immense acclaim during the Romantic era. Hugo's magnum opus, "Les Misérables," remains an enduring testament to his fervent commitment to social justice, compassionately portraying the struggles of the marginalized. Beyond his literary contributions, Hugo's outspokenness against political oppression and support for humanitarian causes solidify his legacy as not just a masterful storyteller but also a tireless champion of human rights.


Among his most famous works :

  • Les Misérables by Victor Hugo (1862) - An essential masterpiece in world literature, this novel depicts the societal injustices and human struggles that defined Hugo's passionate advocacy for social reform.
  • His Life and Work by André Maurois (1956) - Maurois provides a comprehensive overview of Hugo's influential role in the Romantic movement and his contributions as a poet, playwright, and novelist.
  • The Hunchback of Notre-Dame by Victor Hugo (1831) - An enduring classic, this novel weaves a poignant tale of love, injustice, and redemption against the backdrop of medieval Paris, cementing Hugo's legacy as a literary giant.
